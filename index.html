About me

I am a first-year PhD canditate in causal inference and machine learning at TU Delft, the Netherlands, supervised by dr. Jesse Krijthe in the Pattern Recognition and Bioinformatics laboratory. My research interests relate to causality and decision-making with the goal to advance data-driven methods for high-stakes applications, such as medicine.

Originally, I am from Sweden where I finished a BSc in physics and MSc in mathematics at Chalmers University of Technology. My MSc thesis revolved around learning using privileged information in a time-series setting and was performed under the supervision of dr. Fredrik Johansson. In the past have I also, among many things, done research on surrogate-based black-box optimization, worked with Engineers Without Borders Sweden and been a summer intern at NASA Goddard Space Flight Center.
