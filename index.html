About me

I am a first-year PhD candidate in causal inference and machine learning at TU Delft, the Netherlands, supervised by dr. Jesse Krijthe in the Pattern Recognition and Bioinformatics laboratory. My research interests relate to causality and machine learning intending to advance data-driven methods for high-stake applications, such as in healthcare or public policy.

Originally, I come from Sweden where I finished a BSc in physics and an MSc in mathematics at Chalmers University of Technology. My MSc thesis revolved around learning using privileged information in a time-series setting and was performed under the supervision of dr. Fredrik D. Johansson. In the past have I also, among many things, done research on surrogate-based black-box optimization, worked with Engineers Without Borders Sweden and been a summer intern at NASA Goddard Space Flight Center.
