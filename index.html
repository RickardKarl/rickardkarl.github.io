About me

I am a second-year PhD candidate in causal machine learning at TU Delft, the Netherlands, supervised by Jesse Krijthe and Marcel Reinders in the Pattern Recognition and Bioinformatics laboratory. My research interests are at the intersection of causal inference and machine learning where I strive to create more robust methods for high-stake applications, such as healthcare or policy-making. Recently, I have been looking into how to detect confounding, causal model evaluation, and doing sample-efficient estimations.

Originally, I come from Sweden where I finished my BSc in physics and MSc in mathematics at Chalmers University of Technology.
